Albert Einstein

"Imagination is more important than knowledge. Knowledge is limited. Imagination encircles the world."

Albert Einstein was a German-born scientist. He worked on theoretical physics. He developed the theory of relativity.
He received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for theoretical physics. He lived in the United States and became an American citizen in 1940.
He is widely considered one of the greatest scientists of all time. His contributions helped lay the foundations for all modern branches of physics, including quantum mechanics and relativity.
Many scientists only care about their work, but Einstein also spoke and wrote often about politics and world peace. Einstein's family was Jewish, but Einstein never practised this religion seriously.
He liked the ideas of the Jewish philosopher Baruch Spinoza and also thought that Buddhism was a good religion.
